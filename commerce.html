The Rise of E-Commerce

E-commerce evolved from a niche experiment in the early 90s into a defining force in the digital economy. Companies like Amazon (1995) and eBay (1995) leveraged the growing consumer trust in online transactions and created new markets for books, collectibles, and consumer goods.

Key Innovations

  • SSL encryption (1994–95): Made credit card transactions secure.
  • Online payment systems: Early forms of digital checkout processes.
  • User review systems: Pioneered by Amazon, shaping purchase decisions.
  • Online auction culture: eBay’s bidding model revolutionized personal selling.

Amazon (1995)

Started with books; grew into an early blueprint for digital retail.

eBay (1995)

Created a new digital economy centered on auctions and collectibles.

Consumer Trust

Trust grew slowly — skepticism of credit card use persisted until the late 90s.
