Cultural Impact

The commercialization of the web reshaped online identities and behaviors. What began as a decentralized space for experimentation, anonymity, and community gradually became a marketplace where **users were both customers and commodities**.

Major Cultural Shifts

  • Anonymity to Trackable Identity: Cookies and account systems made users legible to corporations.
  • Creativity to Consumption: Personal homepages gave way to commercial platforms and storefronts.
  • New Expectations: Instant customer service, fast shipping, reliable online purchasing.
  • Attention Economy: Websites competed for impressions, leading to more ads & data extraction.

Loss of Early Web “Weirdness”

The wild, experimental early web faded as corporations imposed uniformity.

Rise of Consumer Identity

Users came to be defined by purchasing habits, browsing behavior, and clicks.

Foundation for Web 2.0

Commercial infrastructure enabled the platforms that dominate today.
