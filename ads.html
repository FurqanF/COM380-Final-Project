Web Advertising in the 1990s

Web advertising emerged almost immediately after the WWW opened to the public. The **very first banner ad (1994)** deployed on HotWired became a blueprint for a rapidly evolving advertising economy.

Key Developments

  • 1994: First clickable banner ad (“Have you ever clicked your mouse right here?”)
  • Mid-90s: Ad networks began tracking impressions + click-through metrics.
  • Late-90s: The pop-up ad was invented, widely hated, but massively profitable.
  • Targeting: Early cookies enabled rudimentary behavioral advertising.

Ad Networks

Companies began aggregating ads across multiple sites, creating the first large-scale digital marketing economy.

Pop-Up Era

A symbol of late-90s commercialization — intrusive, aggressive, unavoidable.

Data Collection

Cookies and tracking scripts laid foundation for today's hyper-targeted ads.
